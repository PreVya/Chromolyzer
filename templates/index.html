Colour theory and basics:
Color theory, a foundational aspect of art and design, explores the principles governing the interaction of colors. The basics involve understanding the color wheel, which illustrates primary, secondary, and tertiary colors and their relationships. Complementary colors, found opposite each other on the wheel, create vibrant contrasts, while analogous colors, located next to each other, offer harmonious combinations. Additionally, considerations of hue, saturation, and brightness contribute to the complexity of color interactions. Mastery of these fundamentals empowers artists, designers, and creators to skillfully manipulate colors for expressive and visually impactful compositions.

Applications:
Colors wield profound influence across various domains, serving as powerful tools in design, psychology, marketing, and beyond. In design, they communicate emotions and establish visual hierarchy, while color psychology connects hues with personality traits. Brands strategically employ colors to convey identity and messages, influencing consumer perceptions. In healthcare, colors create soothing environments, and in traffic systems, they ensure clear communication for safety. The pervasive impact of colors underscores their role as dynamic communicators shaping our perceptions, emotions, and decision-making processes across diverse fields.
